There is only one submission, which is not a valid vendor proposal but the original tender document issued by Al-Asima Solutions K.S.C.P. itself. As such, there are no actual vendor proposals to evaluate for compliance, and this constitutes a critical red flag under Kuwait Public Tenders Law No. 49/2016.

The submission fails to meet fundamental legal requirements under Article 45, which mandates a Tender Bond (bid security) in the form of an irrevocable bank guarantee from a licensed Kuwaiti bank for KWD 5,000,000. The absence of this bond results in automatic disqualification. Furthermore, Article 31 requires bidders to be legally registered Kuwaiti entities with proper commercial and Chamber of Commerce documentation — none of which are submitted.

Additionally, Article 35 specifies that bid validity must last at least 90 days post-submission, but without a submitted bond or any proposal, this condition cannot be fulfilled. The complete lack of mandatory documents — including the National Manpower Ratio Certificate (PAM), authorized signatory proof, and commercial license — violates core administrative and legal obligations under Kuwaiti procurement law.

In summary: This is not a bid. It is the RFP. Therefore, no compliant vendor proposal currently exists in the evaluation pool, creating a full legal and procedural red flag. The buyer must reissue or extend the tender to receive actual, compliant bids.
